<p>The Heartbeat dashboard shows which vehicles are active, when they were last seen, and whether they match your policy records. It is the primary tool for identifying <b>VIN leakage</b> — vehicles on the road but not on your policy.</p>
<h2>Header KPIs</h2>
<ul><li><b>Vehicles on Policy</b> — VINs matched to an uploaded policy schedule</li><li><b>Active Vehicles</b> — Vehicles currently pinging telematics data</li><li><b>Miles in Last 30 Days</b> — Total fleet mileage</li><li><b>Miles per Vehicle</b> — Average over the last 30 days</li></ul>
<h2>Identifying VIN Leakage</h2>
<p>Filter for <b>Out of Policy</b> vehicles that have telematics, are moving, and have miles. These vehicles are actively operating but not on your policy — potential VIN leakage. Under MCS-90, you may still be liable.</p>
<h2>Vehicle Management</h2>
<p>Click the <b>three-dot menu (⋯)</b> on any vehicle row to Add to Policy, Remove from Policy, or Pause Pulling Data. Use <b>Upload CSV</b> to bulk-match your full policy schedule against telematics data.</p>
<h2>Heartbeat Trends</h2>
<p>The Heartbeat Trends view shows vehicle utilization over time — first date with miles, last date with miles, and when each vehicle was onboarded. Useful for identifying new vehicles added mid-policy or vehicles that have stopped moving.</p>
